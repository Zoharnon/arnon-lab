    <h3 class="pi-name">Dr. Zohar Arnon</h3>
    <p class="pi-title">Assistant Professor, Avram and Stella Goldstein-Goren Department of Biotechnology Engineering<br>Ben-Gurion University of the Negev</p>

    <p>
      Zohar completed his B.Sc. in Physics and Biology and his Ph.D. with Distinction in Biotechnology,
      both at Tel Aviv University. His doctoral work in the laboratory of Prof. Ehud Gazit focused on
      peptide and protein self-assembly, revealing how simple molecular building blocks can self-assemble
      into complex structures with unique functional properties.
    </p>
    <p>
      He carried out postdoctoral training at Columbia University with Prof. Oleg Gang, where he developed
      expertise in DNA nanotechnology and programmable self-assembly, spatially arranging molecules in 3D
      superlattices with nanoscale precision.
    </p>
    <p>
      Zohar joined BGU in 2026, where his lab focuses on engineering
      <strong>programmable bionanomaterials</strong> using DNA origami, protein and peptide
      self-assembly, and external physical fields. He is also an editor and author of the forthcoming textbook
      <em><a href="https://molecularprogrammers.org/#aomp" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Art of Molecular Programming</a></em>.
    </p>
